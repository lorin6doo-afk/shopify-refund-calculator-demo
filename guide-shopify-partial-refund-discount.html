Shopify refund math

Shopify partial refund discount calculator

When a customer returns one item from an order that used an order-level discount code, Shopify may calculate the refund from the discounted line value. That can leave the customer with less of the original discount on the items they kept.

Open calculator Back to demo

The common example

A customer buys ten items for $500 and uses a $50 order-level discount. They paid $450.

If they return one $50 item, a discounted line refund may be $45. The customer now paid $405 for the $450 of items they kept, which means only $45 of the original discount remains.

A retain-discount refund compares that with refunding the full returned line value, so the customer keeps the original $50 discount on the items they did not return.

What to check before issuing the refund

  • Original order subtotal before discounts.
  • Total order-level discount.
  • Which line items and quantities are being returned.
  • Shopify-style discounted line refund.
  • Retain-discount refund amount.
  • Discount at risk if you follow the default line refund.
  • Tax, shipping, and accounting treatment before finalizing.

Formula used by this calculator

paid = subtotal - discount
retainedSubtotal = subtotal - returnedSubtotal
targetCustomerKeepsPaying = max(retainedSubtotal - discount, 0)
retainDiscountRefund = clamp(paid - targetCustomerKeepsPaying, 0, paid)
discountAtRisk = retainDiscountRefund - shopifyStyleRefund

Important limit

This is an operations helper, not tax or accounting advice. Use it to sanity-check the refund amount and generate an internal note, but verify tax, shipping, payment gateway, and accounting effects before issuing refunds at scale.
